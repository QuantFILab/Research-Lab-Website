Quantitative Finance

Option Pricing

Our lab specializes in cutting-edge research on Option Pricing and Option Trading Strategies. We are deeply invested in exploring innovative methodologies for pricing options and developing trading strategies that capitalize on these insights. Our work spans various risk types inherent in options trading, such as market, volatility, and liquidity risks. By employing advanced mathematical models, computational finance techniques, and econometric analysis, we aim to enhance the understanding and efficiency of options markets.

One of our pivotal areas of exploration is the adaptation and enhancement of option pricing models to reflect more complex market dynamics. This includes extending traditional models to account for features like price jumps and stochastic volatility, which are critical for accurately pricing options and devising effective trading strategies. Our adaptation of the Black-Scholes model incorporates jump components, providing a more nuanced view of option pricing in markets subject to sudden movements.

Our research goals are not only to advance theoretical understanding but also to apply these insights in practical trading contexts. We collaborate closely with industry partners to test and refine our strategies, ensuring they are robust and applicable in real-world scenarios. Additionally, our team actively contributes to academic discourse, publishing our findings in top-tier journals and presenting at leading finance and computational finance conferences worldwide.

The lab's commitment to bridging theory and practice in options trading has led to significant contributions to both the academic community and the finance industry. Through our rigorous analysis, innovative approach, and collaborative efforts, we strive to push the boundaries of what's possible in option pricing and trading strategy development.

Quantitative Risk Management

The Quantitative Risk Management is at the forefront of developing innovative approaches to understanding and mitigating financial risks. Our research encompasses a broad spectrum of risk types, including market, credit, liquidity, and operational risks. By leveraging advanced statistical models, machine learning algorithms, and big data analytics, we aim to enhance the predictive accuracy of risk assessment models and develop robust risk management strategies that can withstand volatile market conditions.

One of our primary research goals is to refine the methodologies for quantifying and managing extreme risk events, with a particular interest in systemic risks that can lead to widespread market disruptions. Our lab is also investigating the effectiveness of various risk mitigation techniques, such as portfolio diversification, derivative instruments, and dynamic hedging strategies, in reducing the potential impact of adverse market movements.

Additionally, our research extends to the exploration of regulatory frameworks and their implications for risk management practices. We are actively involved in developing models that align with regulatory standards such as Basel III and IV, ensuring that our approaches not only provide advanced risk insights but also comply with international regulatory requirements.

Through our research, the Quantitative Risk Management Lab seeks to contribute to the field by publishing our findings in leading academic journals, participating in international finance and risk management conferences, and collaborating with industry partners to apply our research in practical settings.
