Quantitative Finance

Option Pricing

Our research focuses on refining the Black-Scholes model for option pricing, integrating stochastic volatility and jump-diffusion models to better predict financial derivatives' values, including exotic options.

$$\frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2S^2\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} + rS\frac{\partial V}{\partial S} - rV + \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \left(V(S + S', t) - V(S, t)\right) \nu(dS') = 0$$

Our work provides crucial insights for academia and the finance industry, underscoring the need to evolve traditional models to reflect contemporary market dynamics.
