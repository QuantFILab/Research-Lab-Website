Quantitative Finance

Option Pricing

Our lab specializes in cutting-edge research on Option Pricing and Option Trading Strategies. We are deeply invested in exploring innovative methodologies for pricing options and developing trading strategies that capitalize on these insights. Our work spans various risk types inherent in options trading, such as market, volatility, and liquidity risks. By employing advanced mathematical models, computational finance techniques, and econometric analysis, we aim to enhance the understanding and efficiency of options markets.

One of our pivotal areas of exploration is the adaptation and enhancement of option pricing models to reflect more complex market dynamics. This includes extending traditional models to account for features like price jumps and stochastic volatility, which are critical for accurately pricing options and devising effective trading strategies. Our adaptation of the Black-Scholes model incorporates jump components, providing a more nuanced view of option pricing in markets subject to sudden movements.

Our research goals are not only to advance theoretical understanding but also to apply these insights in practical trading contexts. We collaborate closely with industry partners to test and refine our strategies, ensuring they are robust and applicable in real-world scenarios. Additionally, our team actively contributes to academic discourse, publishing our findings in top-tier journals and presenting at leading finance and computational finance conferences worldwide.

The lab's commitment to bridging theory and practice in options trading has led to significant contributions to both the academic community and the finance industry. Through our rigorous analysis, innovative approach, and collaborative efforts, we strive to push the boundaries of what's possible in option pricing and trading strategy development.

Quantitative Risk Management

The Quantitative Risk Management is at the forefront of developing innovative approaches to understanding and mitigating financial risks. Our research encompasses a broad spectrum of risk types, including market, credit, liquidity, and operational risks. By leveraging advanced statistical models, machine learning algorithms, and big data analytics, we aim to enhance the predictive accuracy of risk assessment models and develop robust risk management strategies that can withstand volatile market conditions.

One of our primary research goals is to refine the methodologies for quantifying and managing extreme risk events, with a particular interest in systemic risks that can lead to widespread market disruptions. Our lab is also investigating the effectiveness of various risk mitigation techniques, such as portfolio diversification, derivative instruments, and dynamic hedging strategies, in reducing the potential impact of adverse market movements.

Additionally, our research extends to the exploration of regulatory frameworks and their implications for risk management practices. We are actively involved in developing models that align with regulatory standards such as Basel III and IV, ensuring that our approaches not only provide advanced risk insights but also comply with international regulatory requirements.

Through our research, the Quantitative Risk Management Lab seeks to contribute to the field by publishing our findings in leading academic journals, participating in international finance and risk management conferences, and collaborating with industry partners to apply our research in practical settings.

Computational Finance

The Computational Finance Lab is dedicated to pioneering research in the application of computational methods to financial markets. Our work explores a variety of risk types, including but not limited to market risk, credit risk, liquidity risk, and operational risk. By employing a blend of quantitative finance models, numerical algorithms, and data analysis techniques, we aim to develop sophisticated tools and strategies for risk management, asset pricing, and financial forecasting.

Key to our research approach is the integration of advanced mathematical methodologies to solve complex problems in finance. An example of such a methodology is the Feynman-Kac formula, which provides a link between stochastic differential equations and partial differential equations, offering a powerful tool for pricing derivative securities under various market conditions.

\[ u(t,x) = \mathbb{E}\left[\exp\left(-\int_t^T r_s ds\right) f(X_T) \middle| X_t = x\right] \]

This formula is instrumental in our exploration of derivative pricing and risk management, enabling the application of Monte Carlo simulation techniques and finite difference methods for solving pricing problems.

Our research goals are ambitious, aiming to not only advance theoretical understanding but also to apply these insights to real-world financial markets. We collaborate extensively with industry partners to test and implement our models, ensuring they meet the practical demands of today's dynamic financial landscape. Additionally, our lab actively contributes to the body of knowledge in computational finance through publications in leading academic journals, presentations at international conferences, and partnerships with other research institutions.

The lab's commitment to bridging the gap between theory and practice in computational finance has led to significant contributions to both the academic community and the finance industry. Our work in developing more accurate and efficient computational models for financial analysis and decision-making underscores our leadership in the field and our dedication to enhancing the sophistication and robustness of financial technology.

Algorithm Trading

The Algorithmic Trading Lab is dedicated to the exploration and development of advanced algorithmic trading strategies that leverage computational finance, quantitative analysis, and artificial intelligence. Our research encompasses a wide array of risk types including execution risk, market impact risk, and model risk. Through the use of sophisticated statistical models, machine learning algorithms, and high-frequency trading data, we seek to understand market dynamics deeply and develop algorithms that can efficiently navigate and exploit these markets.

A cornerstone of our research involves modeling financial markets with complexities such as stochastic volatility and unpredictable market jumps. One advanced model we study is akin to the Heston-Bates model, but with enhancements to incorporate jumps in asset prices, enabling more accurate prediction and management of risks associated with algorithmic trading strategies.

\[ dS_t = \mu S_t dt + \sqrt{v_t} S_t dW_t^S + J_t dN_t \]

Where \(dS_t\) represents the asset price change, \(\mu\) is the drift rate, \(v_t\) the stochastic volatility process, \(dW_t^S\) a standard Wiener process, \(J_t\) the jump magnitude, and \(dN_t\) a Poisson process indicating the occurrence of jumps.

Our lab's goals extend beyond theoretical models to the practical application of our algorithms in live trading environments. We collaborate with industry partners to refine our strategies, ensuring they are both innovative and applicable to the current market conditions. Furthermore, our team is highly active in the academic community, contributing to scholarly articles, participating in international finance conferences, and engaging in collaborations that bridge the gap between academia and the finance industry.
